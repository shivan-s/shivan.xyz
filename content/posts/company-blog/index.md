Friday afternoons are reserved for deep dives, where we discuss topics outside our sprint goals, and work in general. In this deep dive, we discussed the idea of a company blog.

The basic premise of this is a "peek behind the curtains" -- going from idea to implemented feature.

It can be quite scary offering ideas. It's scary to be rejected and have that idea shut down. To that I say "1000 misses, 1 hit" -- but that's a topic for another time...

Fortunately, this suggestion was positively received by the attendees.

## Goals of a Blog

One of the reasons highlighted before was for the blog to act as a behind the scenes.

Some other ideas include:

1. For us to slow down and think about things we have built, even if we are busy with work, it is still important to slow down and think.
2. Improve our abilties to communicate as a team
3. Writing is thinking

## Pros and Cons

1. Not everyone in the team will be a fan of this. The lead should decide on what to write about if it's worth sharing. It's up to the person asked to write an article or not. So no one is forced to write articles.

## Planned Implementation

It doesn't have to be perfect -- in fact, it cannot be perfect from the beginning. I suggest we focus on small incremental improvements as time goes on.

Write to an audience that is technical.
